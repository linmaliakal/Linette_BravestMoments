Voicing What I Want To Do With My Life

For the longest time, I had been avoiding the question my dad wanted to ask me about what I wanted to do with my life. I had an idea and I knew what I was passionate about, but I was afraid he wouldn't approve or let me carry out these aspirations. However, I finally forced myself to sit down with him and have the dreaded discussion. Little did I know, he was completely open to it and told me to chase after what I cared about.

Transitioning to High School

8th grade ended off on the wrong foot after I was released from the hospital, so I was eager to start fresh and make new beginnings. But what I was most afraid of was being judged because of the marks I had on my body or for the fact that I had lost so much hair on my head. When I arrived at school, I met some of the most accepting, genuine people who are to this day my closest friends.

Speaking Up

I have always not been the best at speaking out loud and asking questions when I needed the help. I used to be an extremely introverted, shy kind of person. It had been a challenge throughout my grade school years to speak up when my curiosity peaked and not be afraid to ask something despite the fear of feeling inferior or not as intelligent for not knowing. I've grown to be better though at speaking up about my feelings and physical state of mind.
